Alison Davis
Cardiologist
Dr. Alison Davis is a Cardiologist at the Mayo Clinic. Dr. Davis, M.B., Ch.B., D.Phil, received his M.B., Ch.B. in 1965 from the University of Cape Town, South Africa, and his D.Phil degree from Oxford University, England, as a Rhodes Scholar from 1967 to 1970. From 1978 to 1993, Dr. Davis was at Mayo Clinic where he served as Director of the Coronary Care Units with additional commitments to invasive and noninvasive electrophysiology and cardiac pacing. From 1993 to 1998, Dr. Davis was the William Proctor Harvey Teaching Professor of Cardiology and Chief of Cardiology at Georgetown University Medical Center.
